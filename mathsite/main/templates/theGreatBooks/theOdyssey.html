Notes From The Odyssey

As I delve into this book I'd like to state that I have very little knowlegde of Greek mythology, and I think this book has a lot of it. To keep track of things I've listed below a table of all the characters that I come across:

Character Name About
Achilles (Greek man) Son of mortal Peleus and goddess Thetis. King of the Myrmidons and greatest warrior of the Greeks in the battle between the Achaeans and the Trojans.
Pheonix (Greek man) Son of Amyntor and second father to Achilles.
Thetis (Goddess) Mother to Achilles, wife to Peleus and a sea goddess.
Briseis (Trojan woman) Beautiful princess, stolen by the greeks after the Trojan war and given to Achilles. She then falls in love with Achilles.
Patroclus (Greek man) Son of Menoetius. Best friend and companion of Achilles.
Peleus (Greek man) Father of Achilles and king of Phthia.
Hades (God) Son of Zeus and king of the underworld and the dead.
Apollo (God) God of the sun, music, dance, and poetry. Son of Zeus, in favor of the Trojans.
Leto (Goddess) One of Zeus's consorts who bore with him two children: Apollo and Artemis. Goddess of fertility.
Atreus (God) God of war.
Agamemnon (Greek man) Son of Atreus and king of Achaean army.
Clytemnestra (Greek woman) Wife of Agamemnon.
Menelaus (Greek man) Son of Atreus and king of Sparta.
Chryses (Trojan man) The priest of Apollo.
Chryseis (Trojan woman) Daughter of Chryses, captured by the Greeks.
Calchas (Greek man) Son of Thester, the wisest augur who could tell things past, present and to come.
Minerva/Athena (Goddess) Goddess of wisdom, and daughter of Zeus. Also known as Athena and is in favor of the Greeks.
Juno/Hera (Goddess) Wife of Zeus, daughter of Cronus. Also known as Hera, and is in favor of the Greeks. Goddess of love and marriage.
Zeus/Jove/Jupiter (God) King of the Gods, son of Cronus and ruler of the sky.
Priam (Trojan man) The last king of Troy. Father to Hector and Paris (as well as 48 other sons and many daughters).
Hector (Trojan man) Son of Priam and commander of the Trojan army.
Alexander/Paris (Trojan Man) Warrior of the Torjan army, husband to Helen, and brother to Hector.
Nestor (Greek man) Speaker of Pylians, old king of Pylus and Dorium.
Ulysses (Greek man) Greek warrior and captian.
Eurybates (Greek man) A man of Ithaca and servant of Ulysses.
Thersites (Greek man) A soldier of the Greek army in the battle of the Trojan war, and known as a very ugly man.
Helen (Greek woman) Of Troy, most beautiful woman of Greece and daughter of Zeus.
Venus/Aphrodite (Goddess) Goddess of beauty and love. Daughter of Zeus.
Diomed (Greek man) Son of Tydeus. Argive warrior in the battle of the Trojans and Achaeans.
Dares (Trojan man) Priest of Vulcan. Father of Trojan warriors Phegeus and Idaeus.
Hephaestus/Vulcan (God) Son of Zeus, in favor of the Greeks and god of blacksmiths, metalworking, carpenters, craftsmen, artisans, sculptors, metallurgy, fire and volcanos.
Ares/Mars (God) Son of Zeus, in favor of the Trojans and the Roman god of war. Also an agricultural guardian.
Diomed (Greek man) Commander of 80 Argive ships and a great warrior in the battle between the Trojans and Greeks. Son of Tydeus
Glaucus (Greek man) Son of Hippolochus, a Lycian prince, and a warrior who assisted the Trojans. Also friend of Diomed.
Ajax (Greek man) Son of Telamon and king of Salamis. Known as a great warrior and a towering figure.
Teucer (Greek man) Nephew of King Priam, but fought for the Achaeans. A skilled Archer.

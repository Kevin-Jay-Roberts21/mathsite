Linear Algebra: MATH 2270

Linear Algebra is a type of mathematics that deals heavily with matices, vectors and linear combinations. A vector is a collection of numbers and a matrix is a collection of vectors:

\begin{equation*} \text{Vector: } \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \text{Matrix: } A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 8 \\ 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

To write both in proper notation, we write an arrow over the variable if we are denoting a vector and typically the matrices are capitalized. Another example of proper notation would be the following:

If \begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 7 \\ 0 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \text{and} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

Then \begin{equation*} A\vec{v} = \vec{b} \;\;\;\;\;\; \implies \;\;\;\;\;\; \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 7 \\ 0 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
